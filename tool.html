The Living Constitution 2.0

Runtime: Working PROACTIVE: 731 tests passing Full scope: Partial

TLC 2.0 is a constitutional AI-governed, blockchain-runtime-verified research and development tool. It is not a framework document. It is not a set of principles. It is running infrastructure that executes constitutions — formal, evaluable specifications of epistemic invariants — and enforces them during inference, research, and publication.

The architectural insight that makes it unique: a governance runtime and a domain constitution are different artifacts. TLC executes constitutions. Constitutions encode domain theory. The two can be developed, criticized, and published independently. This means a reviewer attacking a domain constitution is not attacking the runtime. A reviewer attacking the runtime is not attacking any individual research finding.

What "Verifiable Confidence" Means

TLC 2.0 does not claim 100% accuracy — no system governed by the TLC Sociotechnical Constitution makes that claim. What it claims is verifiable confidence: every output carries a truth_status that is governed, evidence-bound, and inspectable.

truth_statusMeaning
workingAll acceptance criteria met. Evidence on file. Reviewer approved.
partialSome criteria met. Evidence on file. Scope is broader than what is verified.
draftIn active development. Incomplete.
unverifiedCreated. Not yet tested or confirmed.
plannedArchitecturally specified. Not yet built.

Nothing is claimed that has not been verified. Nothing is verified that has not been evidenced. Nothing is evidenced that cannot be inspected. This is the constitutional core that makes TLC 2.0 usable at every budget tier — from a $100 hobbyist laptop to a $1B Fortune 500 consortium.

Blockchain Runtime Governance — The Architecture

At the bootstrapped tier, governance is enforced by the pre-commit Truth-State Gate: a Python hook that blocks any commit claiming a module is working without evidence on file. At the startup tier, this becomes a tamper-evident append-only audit log. At the enterprise tier, this becomes an independently auditable ledger no single party controls.

This scaling path directly satisfies:

  • EU AI Act (2024) — high-risk AI systems must maintain logs independently auditable by regulators
  • NIST AI RMF 1.0 — GOVERN/MAP/MEASURE/MANAGE at enterprise scale requires records no single party can alter
  • ISO/IEC 42001 — AI management standard requires tamper-evident governance records

The same constitutional principles govern a solo researcher's laptop and a Fortune 500 global consortium. The blockchain layer is the infrastructure endpoint of what TLC already does at every tier below it.
